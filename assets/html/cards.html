Cards

CPU

The "brain" of the computer. It does all the calculations and tasks.

RAM

Part of the memory that helps the computer be fast. It disappears when you turn it off.

URL

It's the "address" of a website. Example: www.conex.com.br

API

A bridge that helps two programs talk to each other.

HTML

The language used to build internet pages.

SSD

A modern and very fast type of memory where files are stored.

Wi-Fi

A wireless connection to access the internet.

HTTP

A protocol used to browse websites. It's at the beginning of the links.

APP

Short for "application," like WhatsApp or YouTube.

Log in

To sign in to a website or system with a username and password.

Log out

To sign out of the system safely.

Set up

To configure a device or an account. Example: "Set up" a new cell phone.

Back up

To make a copy of your files so you don't lose anything.

Scroll down

To roll the screen down to see more content.

Turn on

To switch on the computer, cell phone, or another device.

Turn off

To switch off the device safely.

Plug in

To connect a cable or charger to the device.

Figure out

To understand how something works. Example: “Figure out” how to use an app.

Firewall

A "digital wall" that protects your computer from viruses.

Cloud

A space on the internet to save photos, videos, and documents.

Download

To get something from the internet onto your device.

Upload

To send files from your device to a website or app.

Malware

A type of program that can harm your cell phone or computer.

Bug

An error that makes a system work the wrong way.

Browser

A program used to access the internet, like Google Chrome.

Software

The programs that make a computer work.

Hardware

The physical parts of a computer, like the keyboard and monitor.

Virtual Assistant

A robot that answers questions, like Siri or Alexa.

Voice Recognition

When a machine understands what you say. Example: "Ok Google."

Automatic Translation

A system that translates text from one language to another.

Smart Filter

The AI chooses what to show you based on what you like.

Chatbot

A robot that talks with you through messages, like in customer service.

Recommendation

The AI suggests music, videos, or products based on what you have already seen.

Face Detection

The camera recognizes your face to unlock your cell phone.

Auto-correction

The cell phone corrects words that you type wrong.

Predictive Text

The AI guesses what you are going to type and shows you options.

Teamwork

Working together with other people to reach a goal.

Problem-Solving

Finding solutions when something goes wrong.

Patience with Technology

Being calm to learn how to use new devices.

Always Learning

To keep learning and adapting, even when you are older.

Listen Carefully

To understand instructions well before you act.

Creativity

Thinking of new ways to do things.

Clear Communication

To speak in a simple and easy-to-understand way.

Decision-Making

Choosing what to do, even when you are not sure.

Empathy

To put yourself in someone else's shoes, especially when they are having a hard time.
