Game Objective

The goal of CONEX is to learn about computers and technology in a fun way. Players will get to know important words, common expressions, and acronyms from the digital world. They will also practice skills like patience, teamwork, and creativity. The first one to reach the end of the board wins!

Number of Players

From 2 to 5 players per room.

How to Access the Game

Go to the game's website. One player must create a room and will get a room code. The other players must enter the game and type in the code to join the same room. When everyone is ready, the game begins!

How to Play

The game works like a digital board. On your turn, you spin the virtual wheel and move the number of spaces shown. Depending on the color of the space you land on, an action will happen.

How to Win

The first person to reach the end of the board wins. To do this, you will need to think hard, have luck with the wheel, and learn along the way!

Game Cards

The game cards appear on the green spaces and have:
Acronyms like CPU, RAM, and URL, explained with simple words.
Verbs like log in, turn on, and scroll down, with examples.
Terms like bug, cloud, and firewall, which help you understand daily digital life.
Concepts of artificial intelligence and entrepreneurship, like "virtual assistant" and "decision-making."
Everything is in accessible language with a focus on inclusion!

Tips for Playing Better

Read the cards and questions carefully.
Don't be afraid to make mistakes: the goal is to learn.
Help your friends if they have a question.
Take the chance to practice new computer words.

For Educators or Facilitators

CONEX can be used in classrooms or interactive meetings with adult education students or senior groups. Encourage reading out loud, group work, and learning together. The game does not require typing or complex commands.
