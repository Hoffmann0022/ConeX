About Us

Created with care by IFSul students

We are Eduardo, Lucas, and Felipe, students passionate about technology. During our English II project at the Federal Institute of Southern Brazil (IFSul), we identified a common challenge for seniors: using smartphones in a practical and safe way. That’s how ConeX was born — a board game designed to teach technology in a simple, fun, and accessible way.

FAQ

Is the game really easy for seniors to play on their own?

Yes! ConeX was designed especially for seniors, with large fonts, simple language, and accessible instructions. Even those who have never played a board game before can easily understand it. The manual was created with clear explanations and a friendly layout to ensure a smooth experience.

Who can play ConeX?

The game is suitable for: Seniors learning to use smartphones
Families who want to bond with grandparents in a fun way
Caregivers, therapists, and social educators
Institutions, NGOs, and community groups
It can be played by 2 to 4 people, of any age group.

Who created the ConeX game?

The game was created by Eduardo, Lucas, and Felipe, students at the Federal Institute of Southern Brazil (IFSul), as part of a project in their English II class focused on digital accessibility. Our goal is to help seniors feel more confident using smartphones while promoting autonomy, inclusion, and joyful family moments.

Do I need to download anything to play?

No! ConeX is 100% digital and can be accessed directly from your browser — no installation required. All you need is a phone, tablet, or computer with internet access. The game is lightweight and works even on basic devices.
